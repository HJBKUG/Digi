Robo Wars

Mode: Offline Event
Grade: VI to XII

Witness a high-octane clash of creativity, coding, and control in ROBO WARS: Clash Of Circuits!

AI for Good: Prompting with Purpose

Event: AI for Good
Mode: Offline Event
Grade: VI to XII
Team Size: Individual
Max Teams per School: 1 Participant

AI for Good: Prompting with Purpose is a forward-thinking event where students become changemakers by tackling real-world societal challenges using the power of AI. Participants will showcase their creativity and precision in prompt engineering to guide AI toward producing meaningful, socially responsible outputs aligned with the theme: AI for Good.

General Instructions:
  • Participants will be given a base prompt and its AI-generated output.
  • They must analyze the limitations of the output and refine the prompt based on a specific refinement goal.
  • The objective is to iteratively improve the prompt so that the AI generates a more appropriate, impactful, or creative response.
  • Each participant will receive two refinement goals and will have 5 minutes for each to craft a suitable revised prompt.
  • After submitting the AI chat link, participants will get 10 minutes to develop and explain the reasoning behind their changes.
  • This explanation will be presented to a panel of judges.
  • Participants must bring their own device with internet access (BYOD).
  • Total Time Duration: 30 minutes including pitch preparation.
Judgement Criteria:
  • Prompt Clarity & Precision – 30 pts
  • Creativity & Innovation – 20 pts
  • Alignment with Refinement Challenge – 30 pts
  • Prompt Engineering Rationale – 20 pts

Cubetron: Rubik’s Cube Challenge

Event: Cubetron
Mode: Offline
Grades:
V–VIII (Category 1)
IX–XII (Category 2)
Team Size: Individual
Max Teams per School: 2 Teams

Get ready to twist, turn, and solve! The Interschool 3x3 and 5x5 Rubik's Cube competition is a thrilling test of speed, focus, and problem-solving skills. This competition invites the best young cubers from various schools to compete head-to-head in solving the classic 3x3 and 5x5 Rubik's Cube in the shortest possible time.

Category 1 (Grades V–VIII): 3x3 Cube
  • Students need to carry their own cube (3x3 for rounds).
  • Prior to each round, a scrambler will scramble the cubes.
  • Scrambled cubes will be provided and a limited inspection time will be given before solving.
  • Timings will be recorded using an official timing device such as a Stackmat timer.
  • The timer will start when the participant touches the cube and stop when the solved state is achieved.
Round Instructions:
  • Round 1: Solve a 3x3 cube. Fastest correct solves will be shortlisted.
  • Round 2: Solve a 3x3 cube again in the shortest time to win.
Judgement Criteria:
  • Round 1: Clearance Based on First to Solve
  • Round 2: Speed (50 pts), Solution Accuracy (50 pts)
Category 2 (Grades IX–XII): 5x5 Cube
  • Students need to carry their own cube (5x5 for both rounds).
  • Prior to each round, a scrambler will scramble the cubes.
  • Limited inspection time will be given to observe the scrambled cube.
  • Timings will be recorded using an official timing device like a Stackmat timer.
  • The timer will start when the participant touches the cube and stop upon solving.
Round Instructions:
  • Round 1: Solve a 5x5 cube. Top solvers advance to Round 2.
  • Round 2: Solve another 5x5 cube. Fastest and correct solution wins.
Judgement Criteria:
  • Round 1: Clearance Based on First to Solve
  • Round 2: Speed (50 pts), Solution Accuracy (50 pts)
